Collisions between circles and a vertex both of which are moving in a straight line.

The way to see if the circle hits the vertex is to look at the straight

line through the vertex and with gradient equal to the tangent of the angle

the circle is travelling at and working out if the line intersects the circle.

If it does then the circle hits the vertex if it moves far enough.

Care has to be taken though because the red line might intersect with the circle and the circle moves far enough to hit the vertex but the circle is travelling away from the vertex not towards it.

Checking if the circle is moving in a direction such that it

can't hit the vertex, even if it the red line intersects.

In these two cases the circle moves away from the vertex so it can't hit it.

If the direction of the circle is on the other side of the line, it might hit the vertex.
